Project Overview

The Great War is the first FPS (First Person Shooter) game developed in Afghanistan, built entirely by a single developer using Unity 3D and C#. On this page, you will read the complete story behind the project how the idea started in early 2023, the challenges faced during development, and how those obstacles were gradually overcome. You will discover the technical journey, including the architectural decisions, optimization struggles, and how the build size was reduced dramatically over time.

You will also learn about the creative process behind designing missions, building environments, and balancing visual quality with performance for low end Android devices. Finally, this page shares the future vision of the project including expansion plans, new missions, and the roadmap toward multiplayer support. In a country where game development is nearly non existent due to limited technological infrastructure, low investment in the gaming industry, lack of funding, minimal access to advanced hardware, and limited awareness of modern development tools, building a high quality and optimized 3D shooter was a major challenge. This project was created to prove that innovation is not limited by geography and to inspire a new generation of Afghan developers to step into game development. This project is more than just a game; it represents persistence, growth, and the belief that visions can become reality through hard work and dedication.


Project Metrics

Through careful optimization and relentless iteration, the game’s build size was dramatically reduced, shrinking from 1.4GB down to just 80MB. Every asset, every line of code was refined to ensure maximum efficiency without compromising quality. Performance was a key focus, and even on low-end devices, the game now runs smoothly, achieving an impressive 45+ FPS on average, giving players a seamless and responsive experience. To make the gameplay rich and engaging, 6 fully playable missions were crafted, each polished to deliver unique challenges and excitement. Every improvement, from optimization to gameplay design, reflects the dedication and methodical effort that turned ambitious goals into these measurable achievements.

Performance Engineering

One of the biggest challenges for The Great War was maintaining high visual quality while ensuring smooth performance on low-end Android devices (Android 6+). To meet this goal, I applied a combination of careful optimization techniques and engineering strategies. Through these efforts, the build size was reduced dramatically, shrinking from 1.4GB to just 80MB even as the game expanded from 1 mission to 6 fully playable missions. Performance on low-end devices improved from 15–20 FPS to a consistent 45+ FPS, while loading times dropped from 30 seconds to just 5 seconds when cached, making gameplay seamless and enjoyable. This was achieved by implementing advanced techniques such as object pooling, asset bundles, occlusion culling, sprite atlasing, LOD systems, multithreading, garbage collection optimization, and texture compression. Each optimization not only enhanced performance but also preserved the visual fidelity and immersive experience that define the game.


Core Gameplay Features

The Great War delivers an immersive single-player FPS experience, featuring 6 playable missions with a carefully story-driven structure that pulls players into the heart of the conflict. Players encounter a variety of challenges with three distinct enemy types: ruthless robots, hybrid robot-human enemies, and fully human adversaries, each with unique behaviors and strategies. The game also includes military vehicles such as tanks and helicopters, complete with realistic physics simulation, adding depth and excitement to combat. To enhance the cinematic feel, Unity Timeline sequences are used to craft dynamic cutscenes that bring the story and missions to life. Every element of the gameplay was designed to combine technical precision, engaging combat, and narrative immersion, ensuring a rich and memorable experience.


Advanced Scene & Mission System

The game features an advanced scene and mission system designed to provide smooth, seamless gameplay. When a mission is loaded, the system first checks for built-in scenes, then searches the local cache, and only downloads the required asset bundles if they are missing. Players are guided through the process with a progress UI, keeping them informed while the game efficiently caches the mission for instant future loading. This system ensures that even larger missions load quickly and reliably, maintaining immersion while keeping the experience smooth across devices.


Architecture Design

The game’s architecture was carefully designed to balance performance, scalability, and maintainability. Each mission is managed by a dedicated mission controller, while intermediate caching classes allow data to be shared efficiently between systems. Persistent game data is handled through PlayerPrefs, ensuring long-term storage of player progress, and a singleton Audio Manager provides centralized control over all sound and music. Performance was further improved by reducing reliance on costly GetComponent calls and implementing reference-based communication between systems, creating a responsive and optimized gameplay experience across all devices.

Future Roadmap

Looking ahead, The Great War has an ambitious roadmap aimed at expanding and enhancing the experience for players. Plans include a multiplayer expansion using Photon or Netcode, and additional missions that will introduce 3–5 new story chapters. The game will also see enhanced AI, featuring squad tactics and dynamic difficulty, along with improved visual effects through advanced particle systems and post-processing. A PC port is planned for platforms like Steam and Itch.io, and localization support will bring the game to Dari and Pashto-speaking audiences. These upcoming features reflect a commitment to continuous growth, deeper immersion, and broader accessibility, ensuring that the game evolves into an even richer experience for its players.

Tools & Technologies

To bring The Great War to life, I relied on a suite of industry-standard tools and technologies. The game was built in Unity 3D using C#, with 3D assets created and optimized in Blender. Performance and memory were carefully monitored with Unity Profiler and Memory Profiler, while Asset Bundles and Unity Timeline enabled efficient content management and cinematic sequences. For deployment and version control, I used the Android SDK and Git/GitHub, ensuring smooth development workflows and reliable releases. These tools provided the foundation to deliver a polished, optimized, and technically sophisticated game entirely as a solo developer.
