Droploop Collective is a fully customized Shopify e-commerce website designed to showcase a curated collection of fashion, lifestyle, and creative goods. The project focused on delivering a clean, user-friendly shopping experience with optimized performance, intuitive navigation, and seamless mobile responsiveness. I built the store from scratch using Shopify's Liquid framework while maintaining a lightweight codebase and fast loading times.

The client needed a store that felt premium yet approachable with featured collections, dynamic product grids, and integrated promotional zones. I designed a custom theme with smooth animations, collection organization, and a mobile-first layout. One major challenge was customizing Shopify’s Liquid templates while keeping the store fast. I solved this by optimizing theme assets, implementing lazy loading for all product images, and using CSS grid/flexbox for responsive fine-tuning. Additionally, I integrated product reviews, newsletter sign-up, and upsell sections to maximize average order value.

After deploying Droploop Collective, the brand achieved a modern, cohesive look that perfectly matched their identity. Site speed improved by over 2.5x compared to their previous template, and user engagement metrics increased significantly. The client reported smoother checkout experiences and higher retention thanks to personalized product recommendations. Future improvements include AI-based product recommendations, enhanced analytics tracking, and expanding the website with a creative blog + membership system for exclusive drops.

Challenges & Solutions: One challenge was aligning brand aesthetics across different screen sizes while preserving design integrity. I applied fine-tuned responsive adjustments using CSS grid and flexbox, and tested across real devices. Another challenge was integrating upsell logic without slowing the store I used asynchronous JavaScript to load recommendations after the main content. The final store now loads quickly even on 3G networks, and the client can manage everything through the familiar Shopify admin panel.

Walkthrough

How I Built The Store

I started by analyzing the client's product catalog and business goals. Using Shopify's Dawn theme as a base, I created a child theme and heavily customized Liquid templates, CSS, and JavaScript to match the brand's minimal yet vibrant identity. I structured collections using smart tags and custom filters, making navigation intuitive for creative entrepreneurs. For the homepage, I designed a dynamic hero section with featured collections, promotional banners, and a newsletter popup that triggers after user engagement.

To ensure high performance, I minified all theme assets, implemented image lazy loading via native lazy attributes, and deferred non-critical JavaScript. I used CSS Grid for product galleries and Flexbox for flexible card layouts. I also integrated third‑party apps: Judge.me for product reviews, Klaviyo for newsletter sign‑up, and Rebuy for upsell/cross-sell recommendations. All apps were carefully selected to not bloat the frontend.

The checkout experience was streamlined by customizing cart drawers and adding trust badges. For SEO, I optimized meta fields, created SEO-friendly URLs, and added structured data (product schema). The entire build took 4 weeks from concept to launch, including QA on real devices. Post‑launch, I set up analytics tracking and trained the client to manage collections, blog posts, and promotions without coding. Future improvements will integrate AI-based product recommendations, a creative blog, and a membership system for exclusive drops.
